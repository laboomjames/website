Building in Public: My Journey Learning to Code

Jan 16 2024 • 5 min

When I started the Build Your Own Apps course, I made a conscious decision to document my journey publicly. Here's why that matters and what I've learned so far.

Learning to code is often portrayed as a solitary pursuit. But by building in public, you create opportunities for feedback, accountability, and unexpected connections.

Why Build in Public?

Building in public forces you to clarify your thinking. When you know others might read about your process, you naturally become more thoughtful about documenting decisions and explaining your reasoning.

It also creates a valuable record of your progress. Looking back at earlier projects helps you appreciate how far you've come, while potentially helping others who are just starting their journey.

My First Two Projects

I started with two applications that solved specific problems I encountered:

  • A Cyberpunk-themed breathing exercise app
  • A minimal Pomodoro timer for focused work sessions

Each project taught me fundamental web development concepts while producing something immediately useful.

Looking Forward

As I continue this journey, I'm excited to tackle more complex projects and share what I learn along the way. Building in public isn't just about showing your work—it's about creating a dialogue with others who are on similar paths.
